## Summary

SRT splitting now uses word-level pauses as active split points. If adjacent Doubao words have a gap of at least `0.8s`, M1 can split one utterance into multiple subtitle chunks even when the text has no strong punctuation boundary and is not longer than the normal character limit.

This specifically addresses phrases like:

```text
OK我先啊缩放的小一点哈
同时呢我们再来看
```

where the waveform has a clear pause but the ASR utterance is one sentence.

## Changes

- Added `WORD_GAP_SPLIT_SECONDS = 0.8` in SRT rendering logic.
- `split_subtitle_items()` now tries word-gap splitting before text-based splitting.
- Gap chunks use first/last word timestamps directly and keep `time_source: "word"`.
- Gap chunks are marked with `split_reason: "word_gap"`.
- If any gap chunk is shorter than the minimum subtitle duration or empty after SRT punctuation cleanup, the parent falls back to existing text splitting.
- Transcript and ASR JSON remain unchanged; this affects SRT output only.

## Lesson

Word timestamps solve alignment after a split, but they also need to drive split decisions. For subtitles, audible pauses are often stronger boundaries than punctuation returned by ASR.
