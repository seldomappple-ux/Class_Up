## Summary

Real editing-software validation found that the latest generated SRT has timing accuracy issues. The issue is not a single fixed offset. It combines subtitle readability splitting error and long-duration audio timeline drift.

Tested output:

```text
D:\学而时习之\🚗车🚗渲染——阿多尼斯硕\素材\录播\3-1_Subtitles_2.srt
```

The video was checked as approximately `29.9 fps`.

## Real Test Findings

Early subtitle anchors:

- `U 像这种`: expected around `00:18`, SRT appeared around `00:21`, about 3 frames late.
- `那你基本`: expected around `05:00`, SRT appeared around `04:08`, about 22 frames early.
- `但是`: expected around `16:00`, SRT appeared around `14:28`, about 32 frames early.

Later subtitle anchors:

- Later single-sentence subtitles also appear about `35-40` frames early on average.
- At `29.9 fps`, `35-40` frames is about `1.17-1.34s`.

## Diagnosis

Two separate effects are present:

- Subtitle readability splitting currently distributes one ASR utterance by character count. This creates estimated child subtitle timings, not true speech timings.
- The extracted `full_audio.wav` is shorter than the source MP4 audio stream by about `1.433812s`, which explains the later `35-40` frame early drift.

Measured media durations:

- Source MP4 video duration: `3858.231667s`
- Source MP4 audio stream duration: `3858.159125s`
- Extracted `full_audio.wav` duration: `3856.725313s`
- Missing duration: `1.433812s`, or about `42.87` frames at `29.9 fps`

## Durable Record

This entry is the durable record for the real subtitle timing drift findings. Do not duplicate this incident record under `docs/`.

## Lesson

Subtitle readability and subtitle timing accuracy are different product goals. Reading-friendly splitting can improve visual density but must not be treated as frame-accurate alignment. Long-form media also needs explicit audio timeline validation; monotonic SRT timecodes alone are insufficient.
